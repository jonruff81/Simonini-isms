10 Rules of Business

  1. 1. Find what you are passionate about and do it.
  2. 2. Do what you say you are going to do every time.
  3. 3. Communicate back to everyone in a timely fashion. Even those you do not wish to talk to.
  4. 4. Be on time or early every time. Being late is disrespectful.
  5. 5. Set proper expectations. Under commit and over deliver.
  6. 6. Treat everyone with respect and love.
  7. 7. Solve the problem and not the person.
  8. 8. Be prepared for presentations. Winging it rarely works.
  9. 9. Always have meetings on time and with an agenda.
  10. 10. Do everything with the customer in mind. You have to have sales to stay in business.

Weathertight with Breathability

Masonry cavity wall flashing and weep hole mortar guard drainage system – Properly installed house wrap – Continuous soffit vents – Heal and seal window and door flashing systems – Drip caps at windows – Sealed pan at front door – Oversized steel plates protect water lines – Piped AC condensation line – 5" gutters and 3"x4" downspouts – Through wall flashing used for brick application at chimneys and valleys where lower and upper roofs meet

Solid Construction Feature

Simonini Homes are built with strict structural standards, substantial materials, by respectable tradesmen all following detailed Simonini quality guidelines that includes continuous 12" concrete block wall foundation, cast iron plumbing drops, sound insulation at plumbing lines, OSB sheathing, 1st floor humidifier and more.

Benefit

In addition to proven longevity resulting from solid construction and a wise investment, your Simonini home is solid without the bounce felt in much construction and your home is quiet with reduced sounds from outside to inside and from floor to floor.

Features

Engineered I-Joist 16" on center – OSB sheathing – Brick homes with masonry cavity wall flashing and weep hole drainage system – Cast iron plumbing drops – Pex water lines – Sound insulation for primary bedroom, laundry room and powder room – Architectural shingles – ½" sheetrock screwed to studs – Moisture resistant green board at wet locations – First floor humidifier – Duct work protected from dirt and trash during construction

  • No returns in dining room – multiple smaller returns

  • Create chases

Phase 20-200: Sitework

  1. All temporary drives over sidewalks should have a minimum of 10" of dirt on top of the sidewalk to "bridge" the sidewalk to help reduce cracking of the sidewalk during construction.
  2. Cut down 8" at curb cut for driveway and use ballast stone at temporary drive.

Phase 20-300: Footings

  1. Footings are to be stepped in 8" increments unless otherwise noted.
  2. Tail ditches must be dug on all footings to ensure water is not held in the footing ditch before or after concrete is poured. If rain collects in the footings prior to concrete placement and makes soil unsuitable for proper bearing, re-digging of footing to adequate soil bearing will be at the trade partner's expense.

Phase 20-310: Foundation

  1. All piers must use jamb blocks, so that the piers are smooth blocks on all sides. All cells to be filled solid. Pier sizing is per plan. NOTE: All piers over 4 blocks high must be 16" x 16" minimum.
  2. Crawl access door opening must be a minimum of 36" x 40" to allow for proper access. Verify location(s) with Superintendent.
  3. If foundation has brick, Trade Partner to protect from mud with straw or silt fence.
  4. All mortar joints on the inside of the crawl space must be troweled to smooth.

Phase 20-330: Waterproofing – Exterior Drains

  1. Waterproofing membrane to be placed to top of foundation all the way round regardless of grade.
  2. All waterproofed areas shall have a French drain or strip drain installed at footing-to-wall intersections. All pipes shall be run to daylight and marked with large stake at open end upon completion. Schedule 40 PVC pipe specified and all pipes to be extended a minimum of 15 ft. away from house.
  3. A minimum of two clean-outs shall be installed using rigid schedule 40 PVC pipe. Unless otherwise specified by Simonini superintendent, cleanouts to be located at corners of foundation.
  4. House walls common with the garage require waterproofing and drains when the natural grade is sloping toward this location.
  5. Waterproofing garage walls on slab homes.

Phase 20-335: Sealed Crawl Spaces

  1. Run exterior drain around entire exterior of foundation. Drain must be run to daylight and flagged or staked to prevent damage – minimum of 15 ft. away from house.
  2. Crawl access doors to be insulated steel.
  3. Dehumidification as soon as possible (as required): Recommend use of a refrigeration dehumidifier of a minimum of 15-20 gallons per day during construction. If conditions take on significant bulk water or very high moisture levels of greater than 40% in the wood substrates, then try using electric heat in isolated locations or desiccant dehumidifiers. These units can use significant amps to power, therefore, constant checks of the temp power pole for breaker trips is a must. Additional outlets may be required.

Phase 20-400: Interior Slabs (Garage and Basement)

  1. Lay vapor barrier and insulation per code. Vapor barrier shall be lapped 6" minimum and taped at all seams. Vapor barrier must be placed under all concrete pours.
  2. Expansion joint material and installation shall be in compliance with the drawings and/or building code and installed at all slab edges adjacent to other surfaces.
  3. GARAGE SLAB – Trade to hold concrete flush down so transition at doors is smooth and not raised. To be direct transition – no lip.

Phase 30-100: Framing

  1. Install roof anchors for safety.
  2. Steel shims to be used when shimming beams sitting on masonry.
  3. Structural frame engineer.
  4. Use glue in all hangers during installation.
  5. All sub-flooring to be glued and screwed at the same time 6" o.c. in the perimeter and 8" in the field. All ends must be capped per manufacturer's specifications. Advantech 1/8" gap on ends and T\&G edges are self-spacing.
  6. OSB to be on outside of headers; no box beams or sandwich beams.
  7. Drill holes and flag to mark point loads.
  8. Use 2 x 6 framing on all laundry and pocket doors when possible.
  9. All exterior corners to be "California" corners. All interiors walls that meet an exterior wall will be blocked with "ladder back" blocking to allow for insulation in the exterior wall, unless specifically directed otherwise by the superintendent.
  10. Trade Partner and Superintendent to identify pinch-points on roof. All crickets to extend minimum of 6"-8" past adjacent wall.
  11. When framing doors that are adjacent to cabinets, check with Superintendent for proper clearances to insure countertop and trim space.
  12. A central cut area must be established and maintained throughout home construction. Cut area is to be used so that scrape material can be reused efficiently and minimize framing lumber waste.
  13. A licensed structural engineer may perform a framing inspection in addition to the county inspection and Simonini Homes' inspection. All items needing correction will be corrected by the trade partner per this Agreement.
  14. When using steels I-beams in any floor system, always run ¾" subfloor over steel. Never butt to it and always use heavy glue between steel top and ¾" plywood.
  15. Framer shall exercise due care not to waste material and to keep all material neatly stacked on jobsite. Cover and protect lumber from elements with Poly during construction.
  16. All vertical walls shall have six-sided construction for energy efficiency. This means areas like bonus rooms and tray ceilings will require the installation of Thermo-Ply or other backing material to allow for all insulation to have an air barrier on both sides of the wall.
  17. Door flashing – install 1 x 4 floor system sloping with cedar shims, then install door pan or bituminous barrier over 1 x 4 and up the sides of door jambs 6". Install door pan and nail only the top of the side flanges (additional door pans to be at Superintendent's discretion). Apply overlapping flex wrap and door pan, taping all miscellaneous seams. Then cut into house wrap at top of door and add drip cap. Caulk and double tape sides and top of door flanges only. NO CAULKING UNDER DOORS.
  18. Window flashing – all windowsills to be sloped then fold house wrap into opening. Staple flex wrap on sill, hanging inside of opening 1-1/4" and folding up the sides of the jambs 6". Overlap house wrap on sides of flex wrap. Cut into house wrap and install drip cap. Caulk and double tape window flanges on the sides and top only. NO CAULKING OR TAPE ON BOTTOM OF WINDOWS.

Phase 30-150: Roofing

  1. Trade Partner to review all pinch-points with Simonini Superintendent prior to starting work.
  2. Through-wall brick flashing to have ice/water membrane on top to separate aluminum/copper from steel lintels.
  3. Trade Partner to supply and install through-wall flashing for brick and stone veneers per Simonini specs and details. All through wall flashing that has an angle in contact or bolted to it must be covered with bituthene.
  4. The top of all parapet wall caps and 1' down the wall need to be covered in bituthene and copper soldered and welded.

Phase 30-230: Windows and Doors

  1. All doors should have a door pan installed per manufacturer's installation instructions with all seams 100% welded together to create a watertight pan assembly.
  2. Protecting our thresholds
  3. Sapele door over 5' overhang
  4. Accoya less than 5' overhang

Phase 30-400: HVAC

  1. All units installed in a closed crawl space shall be equipped with isolation pads to reduce vibration. All closed crawl spaces must have 1cfm of air for every 30sf of crawl space based on a 32" average foundation height. Supply air with a backflow damper must be pumped into the crawl space to meet this criteria. Supply air must be distributed such that the air flow eliminates any "dead pockets" of air in the crawl space.
  2. Supply and install a 5" media HEPA filter with a minimum of 11 MERV. Install a barometric or backdraft damper to prevent drafting while unit is not running.
  3. Paint floor vent black and caulk perimeter.
  4. Install steam humidifier in climate-controlled area.
  5. Cover all in-floor ducts during construction. Write duct boot size on cover.
  6. Supply air grills at sliding doors and French doors should be 6" off exterior wall so that draperies do not interfere with air supply.
  7. All supply/return straps to be bent upwards and screwed to side of joist or framing member or block ¼" 3/8

Phase 30-420: Plumbing

  1. On homes with pools Trader Partner to provide supply line with backflow valve and shut off for pool auto fill. Also provide installation and call-in county inspection for backflow valve for pool auto fill.
  2. Supply tub liners for protection in all tubs after tubs have been roughed in.
  3. All exterior wall penetrations and floor penetrations created by the trade partner shall be sealed and waterproofed against air and moisture penetration by the trade partner using Quick Flash system in a manner acceptable to Alan Simonini Homes, LLC.
  4. Protect all cabinets while performing work in and around them.
  5. Water shut-off valves to under-counter ice makers shall be installed inside adjacent cabinet and have typed labels to identify them.
  6. Laundry rooms above finished space must have a floor drain.
  7. All piping shall be installed on interior walls.
    • Piping installation in exterior walls must be brought to the superintendent's attention BEFORE commencing work.
    • All piping in exterior walls must be marked on the exterior house wrap with fluorescent paint.

Phase 30-440: Electrical

  1. All exterior wall penetrations created by the trade partner shall be sealed and waterproofed against air and moisture penetration with Quick Flash in shingle fashion.
  2. Install outlets, switches and hard plastic cover plates (medium size) with screw head slots to be vertical and plates level. Color shall be white unless upgraded color is selected. Verify color with Superintendent prior to trim-out.
    • Cover plates installed on stained surfaces (walls and/or cabinets) shall be wood or plastic and stained or painted to match color of surface.
    • All electrical switches shall be installed to a height of 39" off sub-floor to bottom of box, except around countertops, to keep them in line with the electrical outlets.
    • All electrical switches shall be installed 4.5" to 6" from a door opening or end of wall so as to be as inconspicuous as possible (avoid switches in the middle of walls). Check with Superintendent if not sure.
    • Any exposed wiring inside or under cabinetry where visible needs to be run in conduit.
  3. Individual breakers within break panels shall be labeled with typed, self-adhering labels indicating what they control.
  4. No tools, equipment, or supplies to be placed on countertops or finished floors without protection. Use drop cloths or moving blankets to protect all surfaces. No shoes on any unprotected floors.
  5. Electrical meter base must be mounted to house immediately after exterior veneer is installed. If temporary heat is to be used, coordinate with Superintendent on installation of meter base prior to exterior veneer installation.
  6. All GFCI breakers for any bathroom device must be located in that bathroom or the electrical panel.

Phase 30-600: Insulation

  1. Insulate as specified unless noted otherwise in house-specific specifications.
  2. All 6 sides of insulation in unfinished storage areas to be secured from falling.

Phase 30-700: Masonry

  1. Verify with Superintendent that all through-wall flashing above roof lines, at decks, around windows and doors, etc. has been inspected and accepted by the Superintendent prior to installing any brick.
  2. Install weeps every 24" O/C on veneer walls. Mortar Net or equivalent must be used behind all PVC weep locations. One inch of airspace behind masonry veneers must be kept clear and free or mortar.
  3. Properly tool all mortar for aesthetics as well as to densify joint.
  4. Backside of all masonry work inside crawl spaces must be tooled.
  5. Cover all material and brickwork at the end of the day. Cover all brick work to ensure rainwater does not ruin freshly laid work.
  6. Keep windows, doors, patios, thresholds, etc. protected from mortar.
  7. Remove all mortar/brick debris from ground next to building and place in a location determined by the Superintendent. Place plastic next to foundation with sand on top to prevent brick from being splashed and stained by mud. Add silt fence and straw.

Phase 30-730: Stucco

  1. All chimneys to be wrapped with bituthene or equivalent.

Phase 30-800: Hardwoods

  1. Check sub-floor moisture content. Do not install hardwoods unless moisture reading is between 7% and 10%.
  2. Trade Partner to apply vapor resistive sealer to all concrete slabs.
  3. Pre-finished flooring to be glued down. Use vapor resistive glue or a roll-on vapor barrier product.
  4. Install #15 felt. Under floor with 6" laps and turned up.
  5. RAM Board protection

Phase 30-820: Drywall

  1. Full bathrooms to be moisture resistant drywall on all walls and ceilings.
  2. Remove all screws that break paper and/or miss studs
  3. All work will be performed to a minimum Level 4 finish as defined by the Drywall Finishing Council (Felipe).

Phase 30-900: Exterior Trim/Boxing/Siding

  1. Prime all cut ends of GP or LP Prime Trim boards with Kilz supplied by Alan Simonini Homes, LLC. All wood in in contact with masonry products must be pressure treated or pre-primed.
  2. Block and string lineboxing as part of frame punch.
  3. Install vents (continuous/box/gable). Vents need to have flashing installed in shingle fashion.
  4. Moisture resistant drywall to be used for all porch ceilings.
  5. If using 2x material must be fir and primed on 6-sides. 2x should be wide enough to accept interior and exterior surfaces minimum.

Phase 30-950: Exterior Slabs on Compacted Fill or Suspended

  1. Do not terminate gutters to flow onto porch floor. Get direction from Simonini superintendent for any gutter drains that end at porch floor and accommodate for sleeves.
  2. When concrete slab is on a steel pan, power vents are required.
  3. Wash out concrete pad for Porta John

Phase 40-200: Cabinets

  1. All base and wall cabinets must be clearly laid out and marked on the floor of every home at the completion of framing and prior to ordering any cabinets. Should any conflicts arise Trade Partner must confer with Simonini superintendent prior to making any changes to cabinet layouts.
  2. Supply color match trim.
  3. Install rubber bumps on all overlay doors and drawers.
  4. 90° stops where necessary.

Phase 40-300: Countertops

  1. Verify over counter sink/windows
  2. All countertops must be approved by client.
  3. Clip under sink in lieu of wood blocking.

Phase 40-500: Ceramic Tile

  1. Where a tile surface meets a non-tile surface, caulk all 90° square joints with sanded caulk only. Where tile meets tub, use grout caulk. Use a color that best matches grout color. This includes but is not limited to tile backsplashes to counter tops, tile walls to tubs, and tiled tub platforms to wood floor.
  2. Countertops are to have grout sealer applied.
  3. An "Anti-crack" membrane must be applied on top of all concrete floors prior to setting tile.
  4. No nails at shower pan top or inside curb.

Phase 40-600: Interior Trim

  1. Pocket doors to be painted on the three hidden sides BEFORE installation.
  2. All pocket doors to have "soft" open and close. (No plastic guide, router bottom)
  3. All double doors to have magnet latches at the top.
  4. Trim vendor to sand and wood-fill all joints and nail holes.
  5. All joints in wood trim to be biscuit-joined and glued.
  6. All joints in MDF trim to be glued per manufacturer's recommendations. Use 2-part glue on MDF/wood glue on wood.
  7. All trim must be nailed into studs or joists. Do not nail into OSB, drywall or Durock wall sheathing without finding the stud first. There may be plumbing or HVAC Lines behind the wall sheathing, and Trade Partner will be responsible for any nails subsequently discovered to be penetrating those lines.
  8. Build and install attic access doors. Install pull-down stairs per manufacturer's instructions (screw unit to frame, no nails). Trim to cover bolts on pull-down stairs and cut shims back.
  9. Build and install wood pad for mounting of garage door push buttons and hose bib.
  10. Use screws at garage wood posts, not nails.
  11. All unused material must be stacked neatly on the trim rack, so that it may be returned for credit. Notify Superintendent of any material needed to finish or punch-out the home.

Phase 50-150: Driveways and Walks

  1. Supply and install a 4" PVC pipe as directed by Simonini superintendent under driveway and walk and also to any areas that shall be rendered inaccessible to future underground lines. Pipes to be buried under control joints a minimum of 12" below top of sub-grade. Fill and compact with mechanical tamp. Mark locations of pipes either in the concrete or leave stubbed up at 45° angle above ground.
  2. Install 4" PVC pipe under curb/driveway and driveway/garage expansion joints for electrical wiring.
  3. No utilities in concrete. If utility boxes have been placed in driveway, get direction from Simonini's superintendent before pouring.
  4. Final grade driveways with either crushed stone or pit gravel. Pit gravel should be compacted to 95% compaction. All driveways shall be proof rolled in the presence of the superintendent. All soft spots shall be repaired prior to pouring.
  5. Straight line where drives meet city roads.

Phase 50-200: Pools

  1. All pools to be equipped with autofill and gravity drain bypass for overflow.
  2. Paint pipes black at all equipment.

Phase 50-400: Wood Decks

  1. Install proper flashing behind all wood attaching to the home.
  2. Use peel & stick tape or similar on top of all framing joist bands.

Phase 50-500: Landscaping and Irrigation

  1. Install minimum 4" PVC sleeves under drives and walks for irrigation lines, electrical, etc. Depth of sleeves shall be 12 inches from top of pipe to bottom of concrete. See Simonini superintendents for locations.
  2. Trade partner to lay ¾" plywood on concrete drives and walks for traffic and machinery.
  3. Add river rock or similar around all daylight drains.
